Why it works


Techniques in Action Today

Using WattTime, electricity consumers can optimize their electricity use to promote the development of cleaner energy sources while lowering greenhouse gas emissions.

Percent Clean Energy

WattTime shows people how much of their region's electricity comes from clean and renewable sources. This is one of the most effective ways to shift demand towards economically sustainable fuel sources.

Marginal Fuel

With the ability to see current marginal fuel sources in their region, electricity consumers can shift their demand as they like towards more renewable fuels such as hydropower and biomass away from dirty sources such as coal and oil. (Currently implemented in New England only; additional regions coming soon).

Curtailment

By exploiting wind power oversupply, electricity customers in select locations will be able to reduce indirect emissions by up to 100% at optimal times.

Renewable Intermittency

Device automation allows for fine-grained energy response timing. It can leverage minor spikes in the availability of renewable energy and help make renewable energy more competitive with fossil fuel-powered plants like coal.

Reduced Ramping

With automation, users can even reduce pollution from traditional power plants by allowing them to reduce wasteful episodes of turning up and down repeatedly. Some estimates suggest this technique can reduce power plant emissions by up to 25%.

Forecast Insurance

WattTime's Renewables Integration Response tools can help offset the difference between actual wind power plant production and wind power forecasts by moderating consumer demand during the most valuable moments. This increases grid stability and paves the way for renewables to compete on equal footing in capacity markets.
