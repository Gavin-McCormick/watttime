Impact


Impacts in Action

Using WattTime, electricity consumers can optimize their electricity use to promote the development of cleaner energy sources while lowering greenhouse gas emissions.

Percent Clean Energy

WattTime shows people how much of their region's electricity comes from clean and renewable sources. This is one of the most effective ways to shift demand towards economically sustainable fuel sources.

Marginal Fuel

With the ability to see current marginal fuel sources in their region, electricity consumers can shift their demand towards more renewable fuels such as hydropower and biomass away from dirty sources such as coal and oil. (Currently implemented in New England only; additional regions coming soon).

Coming soon

By combining open data with our Renewables Integration Response analytics, WattTime is working to equip ordinary people with powerful, real-time economic instruments. (These are currently under development by our crack team of economists and data analysts from UC Berkeley, MIT, and the University of Western Australia.)

Curtailment

By exploiting wind power oversupply, electricity customers in select locations will be able to reduce emissions by up to 100% at optimal times.

Renewable Intermittency

Device automation allows for fine-grained energy response timing. It can leverage minor spikes in the availability of renewable energy and help make renewable energy more competitive with fossil fuel-powered plants like coal.

Reduced Ramping

With automation, users can even reduce pollution from traditional power plants by allowing them to reduce wasteful episodes of turning up and down repeatedly. Some estimates suggest this technique can reduce power plant emissions by up to 25%.

Forecast Insurance

WattTime's Renewables Integration Response tools can help offset the difference between wind power plant actual production and forecasts by moderating consumer demand.
