Impact


Impacts in action today

With WattTime, users can time their electricity use to help cleaner energy sources compete and grow.

Percent clean energy

Our software can show users what share of their region's power currently comes from clean renewable sources, currently the only way for ordinary people to shift demand to prioritize existing inframarginal renewable fuel sources.

Marginal fuel

Users can see the current marginal fuel source in their region, allowing them to shift demand as they like, such as towards cleaner peaker fuels like hydropower and biomass, and away from dirty sources such as coal and oil. (Yes, in today's low natural gas price regime, we do indeed see peaker coal.) Currently implemented in New England only; additional regions coming soon.

Coming soon

By combining open data with automated real-time economic analysis, it should soon be possible for our users to also time electricity to take advantage of a growing list of even more powerful strategies to increase renewable power competitiveness and reduce system emissions without reducing power availability. (Currently under development by our crack team of energy economists and data analysts from UC Berkeley, MIT, and the University of Western Australia.)

Curtailment

By exploiting periods of wind power oversupply due to transmission constraints, users in select locations will be able to reduce indirect emissions by up to 100% at optimal times.

Renewable intermittency

Through automation of select devices, users will be able to use energy at times which exploit minor fluctuations in the availability of wind and solar energy, turning the tables on renewables intermittency and making renewables more competitive with fossil power plants.

Reduced ramping

With automation, users can even reduce pollution from traditional power plants by allowing them to reduce wasteful episodes of turning up and down repeatedly. Some estimates suggest this technique can reduce power plant emissions by up to 25%.

Forecast insurance

By pushing electricity demand to offset the difference between wind power plants's production and their forecats, users can effectively increase the capacity factor of intermittant resources for the few most valuable moments the year, increasing grid stability and paving the way for renewables to compete on equal footing in capacity markets.
