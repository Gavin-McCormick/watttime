General

Are you a private company?

WattTime is a newly-formed nonprofit (501(c)(3) status pending). We also plan to launch a for-profit venture so that we can better expand our capability to help energy consumers make decisions according to their own values. No matter what our legal status is, we will always be devoted to our environmental mission and to our community.

WattTime is "in beta"—what does that mean?

As far as we have been able to determine, WattTime is pursuing a fundamentally novel approach to fighting climate change. While our approach works according to economic theory, we never fully trust theory until it is backed up by hard data and experience. (And neither should you!) By participating in our beta tests, you will help validate the concept and provide critical insight for scaling WattTime to improve the future of energy for yourself and millions of other users. We hope that the excitement of being one of the first to use WattTime's products and services will make up for any occasional glitches that may occur; we'd love to hear your feedback about what's working and what's not.

My organization is interested in partnering with WattTime. What next?

Fantastic, we're excited to hear from you! Please get in touch.

Does WattTime have any new products or services in the works?

Yes, yes we do. For example: an iPhone app is under active development, there's a long list of regions we plan to expand to, major breakthroughs in energy economics are on the horizon, and we're investigating a variety of ways to use WattTime data to control the smart devices in your connected home or work (aka "Internet of Things").

I’m having a problem. How do I troubleshoot?

You can reach us via the contact page. We’re happy to help! Or you can read the source and even submit a patch if that's how you roll.

Alerts

What does the WattTime Alerts cost?

WattTime Alerts is free! Please note however that WattTime Alerts can send occasional text messages (you control the frequency), so if you pay to receive and send text messages, normal rates may apply. Please check with your mobile carrier.

Is WattTime Alerts available in my region?

Currently Alerts by SMS are available in California and New England (Massachusetts, Conneticut, Rhode Island, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine). Morning Forecast Alerts by email are also available in California. If you'd like to be notified when WattTime Alerts become available in your area, just sign up for an account! In the meantime, if you live in the Pacific Northwest or the Midwest, you can see how clean you electricity is at any time by visiting WattTime Explorer.

How do I unsubscribe or change my settings?

To change your settings, including unsubscribing from Alerts, just go to your profile and edit your preferences.

Why do you want to know about my appliances?

Our goal is to give you maximum control over the environmental impacts of your own energy use in as convenient a fashion as possible. Different kinds of appliances are convenient to control in different ways—for example, sometimes laundry can wait until tomorrow, but you probably wouldn’t want to turn off your thermostat for a whole day! The more we know about your appliances, the more convenient our messaging can be, and the more relevant to you. But we’re only asking to help tailor your experience. If you’d rather not tell us about your particular equipment, you are welcome to opt for the generic messages instead.

I signed up for texts about once per (hour/day/week), but I haven’t gotten exactly that number. What gives?

We contact you when the grid is particularly clean or dirty, but of course we can't control exactly when that will be. In fact the electricity fuel mix can be quite unpredictable, depending on everything from weather to the strategies of different power plants. (Otherwise we could just hand out calendars!) We’ll do our best not to text you more than about the amount you signed up for, but some days the grid is pretty average all day and there’s just not much to report. You can always see the recent status of your grid using WattTime Explorer.

In the meantime, if you live in New England, the Pacific Northwest, or the Midwest, you can see how clean your electricity is at any time by visiting WattTime Explorer.

How do I track the impact of my shifted energy use?

Check out your profile! Note that these stats include every time you've used WattTime Shift when you were logged in, and only the times when you were logged in; more accurate impact tracking coming soon.
