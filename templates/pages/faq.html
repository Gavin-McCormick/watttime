General

Are you a private company?

Right now we are just a small group of dedicated individuals trying todo some good in the world on our own time. We may eventually incorporate if we conclude that is the best way to ensure that we can continue to make our service relevant and effective. But our top priority is and will remain to help energy consumers make decisions according to their own values. We believe that when people can freely choose their energy sources it can have a big impact in the fight against climate change.

Why do you need pilot testing?

Every good product and service needs prototyping, iteration, and testing to be the best it can be. As far as we have been able to determine, this is a fundamentally novel approach to fighting climate change. While ours is not the first project to give users information about the cleanliness of electricity, our approach is fundamentally different “under the hood” because it leverages real-time marginal rather than average environmental information, dramatically increasing the impact of your actions. While our novel design works according to economic theory, we never fully trust theory until it is backed up by hard data and experience. (And neither should you!) Our pilot testing and research will help validate the concept and provide critical insight for scaling WattTime to improve energy futures for millions of users.

Alerts

What does the WattTime Alerts cost?

WattTime Alerts is free! Please note however that WattTime Alerts can send occasional text messages (you control the frequency), so if you pay to receive and send text messages, normal rates may apply. Please check with your carrier.

Is WattTime Alerts available in my region?

Currently Alerts by SMS are available in California and New England (Massachusetts, Conneticut, Rhode Island, Vermont, New Hampshire, and Maine). Forecast Alerts by email are also available in California. If you'd like to be notified when we are available in your state, just sign up on our main page! In the meantime, if you live in California, New England, or the Pacific Northwest, you can still see how clean electricity is in your region at any time by visiting our status page.

How do I unsubscribe or change my settings?

To change your settings, including unsubscribing from Alerts, just log in and edit your preferences.

Why do you want to know about my appliances?

Our goal is to give you maximum control over the environmental impacts of your own energy use in as convenient a fashion as possible. Different kinds of appliances are convenient to control in different ways—for example sometimes laundry can wait until tomorrow, but you probably wouldn’t want to turn off your thermostat for a whole day! The more we know about your appliances, the more convenient our messaging can be, and the more relevant to you. But we’re only asking to help tailor your experience. If you’d rather not tell us about your particular equipment, you are welcome to opt for the generic messages instead.

I signed up for texts about once per (hour/day/week), but I haven’t gotten exactly that number. What gives?

We contact you when the grid is particularly clean or dirty, but of course we can't control exactly when that will be. In fact the electricity fuel mix can be quite unpredictable, depending on everything from weather to the strategies of different power plants. (Otherwise we could just hand out calendars!) We’ll do our best not to text you more than about the amount you signed up for, but some days the grid is pretty average all day and there’s just not much to report. You can always see the recent status at our grid status page.
