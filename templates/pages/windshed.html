Congested grids

America's aging energy infrastructure makes it hard for wind farms to compete. One problem is "traffic jams" on the grid,
when energy can't get to where it can be used.

Wasted wind

When wind farms are stuck behind those traffic jams,
lots of their free clean energy gets wasted.
This is bad for wind farms and bad for the environment.

Human potential

People in windsheds can help by using energy at just the right time
with smart thermostats and other smart grid technology.
Environmentally-conscious communities make great targets for pilot projects.

Future windsheds

So many more wind farms are possible!
Community wind integration can make clean energy more financially sustainable,
leading to future wind investments and a cleaner world.

Smart Grid Intelligence

Sustainability programs managers often make difficult decisions about where they should invest in incentives for smart grid technology adoption. Demand response devices like thermostats, smart plugs, and electric vehicle chargers can pay for themselves by improving the market demand for wind energy.

Climate Committed

Windshed maps the best locations for smart grid technology adoption and incentive programs by showing where intelligent electricity timing can stimulate high impact economic incentives for wind energy. This has the added co-benefit of reducing CO2 and other greenhouse gasses.

Network Prowess

To accomplish this feat, Windshed creates the network analytics necessary for leveraging electricity consumers as a "network bridge" between the legacy electricity grid and emerging wind power generation stations. As part of a Renewable Integration Response program, Windshed helps consumers expand their electricity supply options while helping wind farm and electricity grid operators span infrastructure gaps and balance the weak ties of costly and wasteful network congestion with excess wind farm production.
